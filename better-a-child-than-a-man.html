Better a Child than a Man

Kevin

As our body shapes and mind expands, We may overlook things time left behind.

We improve our knowledge and strengthen our thinking. We develop our intuition and sharpen our reasoning. But the child often knows what the man forgets. The child possesses qualities that the man hopes to get.

The child knows to trust, but the man learns to doubt. The child knows to forgive, but the man learns to grudge.

The child doesn’t care for appearance or act what he is not. He is happy with very little and doesn’t greed for a lot. He is simple, humble, joyous and likable. Isn't that what we always wanted? Isn't that what we already had?
