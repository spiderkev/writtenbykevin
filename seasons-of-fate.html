Seasons Of Fate

Kevin

It grows, from a stem on a branch of a tree, after the cold but before the heat. It thrives, in the warmth of the sun, and becomes a fresh green leaf. It matures, into a shade of an autumn color, while the nights grow long and cold. And it falls, to the earth by its foretold fate, before the clouds let go of the snow.

It drops, to the ground in wisps of white, condensed crystals of water vapor. It settles, and forms a blanket of snow, which stays for the rest of the winter. It melts, to slush when spring comes around, and finally disappears, to the air or the ground.

Its Morning, then Afternoon, then Evening, then Night. Its the Seasons of fate, in the circle of life.
