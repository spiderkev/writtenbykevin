Stages of Spirituality

Kevin

The first stage is Chaos; when people pretend to care, to cover up for principles that aren't really there.

They pretend to love, and they think it's genuine; but their relations with others are fake, and self-serving.

Some may be quite disciplined, and succeed in their profession; but they remain unprincipled, governed by their own will.

Many advance, to the stage of Institutions, when they submit themselves to its laws and principles.

Dependent on the institution, they do as their told; but they often focus on its means, and forget its goals.

They are threatened by those who think differently. They have a “us against the world” mentality.

Some advance, to the stage of Skepticism, when they demand ideas that are logical and tangible.

They are genuinely pro-social, though individualistic; which distinguishes them, from the stage of the chaotic.

They are skeptical, and don't believe everything they have read. They dismiss being institutionalized, in order to be saved.

They are abstracted from illusions, and detached from institutions. They don't live in extremes, but somewhere in between.

However, they live in a dilemma between mysticism and science, and they can't decide on where their heart is.

Some continue their seeking, and a few find it, and transcend to the stage of Enlightenment.

They transcend their mind, dilemmas and inhibitions; their self, cultures, religions and institutions.

They find bliss, instead of walls they had thought to exist, and see beauty, in the notion of a one world family...

P.S. People can often be in flux; they can advance or backslide, so this shouldn't be taken intently, it only serves as a rough guide...

Inspiration: Book: "People of the Lie," by M. Scott Peck
